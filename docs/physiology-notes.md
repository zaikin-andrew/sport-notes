# Physiology Notes

## Adaptation Logic

Training creates stress. Recovery creates adaptation.

The athlete should not chase fatigue. Fatigue is a cost, not the goal.

## Aerobic Base

Aerobic base supports:

- mitochondrial density
- fat oxidation
- capillarization
- lower HR at given pace/power
- better recovery between harder sessions
- durability for long events

## Threshold Work

Threshold work is useful but costly.

Use it when:

- easy volume is stable
- recovery is good
- no injury signs
- sleep is acceptable
- the week has room for a hard stimulus

## Long Sessions

Long runs and long rides build durability, but they create systemic load.

Monitor:

- HR drift
- fueling quality
- next-day fatigue
- sleep quality after session
- soreness lasting more than 48 hours

## Immune System Considerations

Risk increases when high training load combines with:

- poor sleep
- calorie deficit
- dehydration
- psychological stress
- insufficient carbohydrates around long or hard sessions
- rapid load increases
